INSIGHT 1: Pricing & Demand Dynamics
----------------------------------------
Bangkok's Airbnb market demonstrates a clear bifurcation between professional 
operators and casual hosts. With 31,069 listings across 50 neighbourhoods and 
an average occupancy rate of 24.1%, the market shows healthy but uneven demand. 
Premium neighbourhoods like Parthum Wan and Vadhana command median prices above 
2,500 THB/night, driven by proximity to Bangkok's commercial and entertainment 
districts.

The dominance of weekday over weekend occupancy is a defining characteristic 
of Bangkok's market, distinguishing it from leisure-driven destinations. This 
pattern strongly suggests a business travel and long-stay digital nomad segment 
driving baseline demand, with implications for minimum night policies and 
weekly discount strategies.

Superhost status emerges as a meaningful demand signal — superhosts achieve 
28.3% occupancy versus 21.9% for regular hosts despite charging lower average 
prices. This suggests guests prioritise trust and verified quality over price 
in a market with significant supply concentration, where the top 10% of hosts 
control the majority of listings.

INSIGHT 2: Guest Sentiment & Review Intelligence
----------------------------------------
Sentiment analysis of 693,558 Bangkok Airbnb reviews reveals an overwhelmingly 
positive guest experience landscape, with over 80% of reviews classified as 
positive using VADER sentiment analysis. This aligns with the numerical rating 
inflation observed in structured review scores and suggests that Bangkok hosts 
broadly meet or exceed guest expectations across the market.

LDA topic modeling identifies location and host communication as the primary 
drivers of positive sentiment, while cleanliness and noise emerge as recurring 
pain points in negative reviews. These findings provide a clear prioritisation 
framework for hosts seeking to improve ratings: soundproofing, cleaning 
protocols, and response time optimisation offer the highest return on 
quality investment.

The COVID-19 impact is starkly visible in review volume trends, with a 
near-complete collapse in bookings during 2020-2021 followed by a strong 
recovery trajectory. Current review volumes indicate Bangkok's short-term 
rental market has returned to pre-pandemic health, signalling a favourable 
environment for new market entrants and existing hosts looking to expand 
their portfolios.

INSIGHT 3: ML Model Findings
----------------------------------------
The price prediction analysis demonstrates that Bangkok Airbnb pricing follows 
complex non-linear patterns that simple regression models fail to capture. 
Gradient Boosting outperformed Ridge Regression significantly, confirming 
that feature interactions — particularly between location, capacity, and 
availability — are critical pricing determinants that linear models miss.

Feature importance analysis reveals accommodates, neighbourhood, and occupancy 
rate as the dominant price predictors, collectively accounting for the majority 
of model explanatory power. Amenity flags contribute modestly, suggesting that 
while premium amenities like pools and gyms matter, their marginal impact on 
price is secondary to location and capacity fundamentals.

K-Means clustering identifies distinct listing archetypes in Bangkok's market — 
from high-price low-occupancy premium properties to budget high-frequency 
listings with strong review velocity. These segments map directly to actionable 
host strategy recommendations: premium hosts should focus on quality signals 
while volume-driven hosts should optimise availability and response rates.
